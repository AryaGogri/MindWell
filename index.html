Warning Signs

When to Seek Help

Recognizing the signs that someone needs immediate support

Suicidal Thoughts

Talking about wanting to die, feeling hopeless, or having no reason to live. Making plans or looking for ways to end one's life.

üòî

Severe Depression

Overwhelming sadness, withdrawal from friends and activities, dramatic mood changes, or feeling trapped.

üò∞

Extreme Anxiety

Panic attacks, constant worry that interferes with daily life, or physical symptoms like chest pain or difficulty breathing.

üí¢

Aggressive Behavior

Sudden rage, threatening violence to self or others, or destructive behavior without concern for consequences.

üç∫

Substance Abuse

Increased alcohol or drug use as a coping mechanism, especially combined with other warning signs.

üîÑ

Behavioral Changes

Dramatic personality changes, giving away possessions, saying goodbye to friends and family, or putting affairs in order.

How to Help Someone in Crisis

Listen Without Judgment

Give the person your full attention. Let them express their feelings without trying to fix things immediately. Sometimes people just need to be heard.

Take Them Seriously

Never dismiss thoughts of suicide or self-harm. If someone is talking about ending their life, take it seriously and get help immediately.

Stay With Them

If someone is in immediate danger, don't leave them alone. Remove any means of self-harm and call emergency services right away.

Connect Them to Help

Encourage them to call a crisis line, talk to a therapist, or visit an emergency room. Offer to help them make the call or go with them.
